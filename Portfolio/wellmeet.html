Problem Overview

Problem

Campus residents are lacking in connections or bonding within their neighborhoods.

Lacking a sense of belonging

From my own experience as a resident in my dorm, I find it hard to get to know other residents. There are also few channels to find likely-minded people to be friends or have groups for activities engagements.

Passive Communication

Residents tend to get information passively and this renders the communications in the community to be mostly in one-way without having more interactions or deep connections with other neighbors.

Research

Competitive Analysis

In this stage, I compared four apps (Nextdoor, Neighborhood Social, NeighborHood, and Vehola) and got many insights and findings. In short, they had similar and quite complete features but still owned less active users. In addition, three of them did not do authentication when a new member requires joining the virtual community. Lastly, the accuracy of information was low as there was no review system to filter biased information.

Storyboard

There are many details popped up especially when I created 5 workflows for each of my solutions. In the storyboard, I utilized what I learned from Dow’s (2010) reading—creating multiple alternatives in parallel would have an overall better performance. I successfully got some ideas from comparing and contrasting the flows I created in the storyboard.

Storymapping

I did a story mapping to note the vivid scenarios down. From the level of activities, tasks, and stories, I thought in detail of user needs and the according features. In doing so, my thinking expanded and converged again and again. I found that my users' profiles are getting more concise and solid, which further helps form design decisions.

Persona

According to the findings from my research, I built three personas, which were two campus neighborhood residents and one residence hall manager, to describe target users and specify their needs.

Ideation

Focus

How to make residents get motivated to constantly engage in their community?

Strengthen Neighborhood Coherence

From the research, I was aware that to build trust, connecting neighbors with daily life activities is a smart strategy. Also, privacy for the whole community should be treated carefully as a lot of private and personal information comes with neighborhood-interaction.

Explore corresponding features
Stick Neighbors on “something"

I got an eye on how to keep users on the platform. Users only stay if the system does well on making intentions and giving motivations to see what’s going on in their neighborhoods. Also, curiosity could be employed in knowing neighbors who share the same interest with you.

Connection

I designed a neighbor matching system, by which users will be given recommended neighbors from the system by the interest fields that they have filled out when registered the account.

Users will be introduced two neighbors once a week. Also, down this page, the system provides the number of neighbors the user had met. This is calculated by the number of events that the user have attended and is a mechanism to encourage users to not only get to know people online but also have more physical connection pushing by WellMeet.

The goal of this “exploring friend” function is particularly for academic network building, as residents in the neighborhood are mostly students or professional researchers. I would love to see interdisciplinary ideas exchanging and future connections either in academic fields or careers!

Activity

There are two approaches for joining an event on this application. Users can browse the activity information posted by the manager of the residence hall and register for events through one click.

In addition, to create motivation and provide a unique experience for the interactions among neighbors, the application allows users to join an interest group, for example, groups for study, sports, eat, or hang out. After that, group members can discuss what they are going to engage together, or they can complete the task assigned by the system according to their interest field. For instance, in the sports group, there will be tasks like go skating, go playing basketball or do yoga together.

Recommendation

The third function is a place where neighborhood information can be stored and enabled to provide easy access. Neighbors can share and review information about the local business such as supplies, restaurants, drug stores…etc. By the mechanism of collaborative review, the community is expected to form a sense of cohesion and furthermore, build their unique culture.

Impact

My design offers the opportunity and increases motivation for campus residents to engage more in their neighborhood. I believe that when more and more people are willing to share their interest and support each other in the neighborhood, they will be able to establish a unique culture, especially for their local community. For an individual user, building up networks for daily life company or for academic/career connection relied on the physical proximity is a helpful and effective strategy. For people new to the local area, they can benefit from my design as it helps residents blend into local neighborhoods and have access to information that make them set up their lives more easily. In conclusion, I created this mobile app in the hope of providing unique living experience and style for campus residents.
