Research Process

Problem

"The process of making student progress report takes forever"

-----Our client, a k5 private school in Michigan has identified the amount of time and effort it takes to produce a single progress report.

Methodologies

Interviews

Starting from September 2017, we conducted background research reports and interviewed a total of eight members of the school community in the hopes of understanding the main problem(s) with the progress report procedure. Upon completing all the interviews, we divided the interviews amongst ourselves to create affinity notes, which are statements that describe each minute of the audio-recorded interviews.

Affinity wall

There was a total of 257 affinity notes, with 57 statements produced during the first round of clustering, 17 statements during the second round, and five overarching statements during the last round. The creation of the affinity wall helped us understand the main problems in the progress report system.

Survey

We also conducted surveys for teachers and parents to gain more data to support our findings. We found that parents usually don't spend much time on the report, however, they still expect updated information from teachers regarding their children's learning performance.

Findings

Inconsistencies lie in teachers' workflow of producing a progress report

There are various different ways in which teachers use to communicate with each other on observations and assessments of students’ academic and social-emotional behaviors. This lack of standardized communication procedure creates a confusing environment in which teachers must parse their way in order to write the progress reports.

The lack of a standardized format for the narrative part of the progress report

Teachers are worried about the flow of writing, word choices, and the structure when writing a progress report. There is no standardized way to write the progress reports, causing teachers to figure out other ways to save time. Also, some teachers expressed technological difficulties in documenting and formatting.

Recommendation

Short-Term

(1)Adding visuals (e.g. infographics, bar graphs, line graphs), either partially or completely, in the place of the textual narrative is another feasible solution.

(2)Implementing a standardized procedure to determine the composition of the progress reports and to evaluate the performance of the progress reports on a regular basis.

Long-Term

Investing in software that auto-generates the structure of progress reports. The school could use a software, such as an education management system, that autogenerates the structures of the progress reports. It can only benefit the school to slowly invest time and finances to develop a more online system that will help teachers and administrators focus on other aspects of their responsibilities.

Design

An in-time communication system which supports quickly update of student's learning performance.

Freya

According to the needs and expectations we gathered from the school community, I decided to build a Drupal site which allows teachers and parents to communicate and keep track of student's learning progress. I have added some customized functions utilizing Drupal modules, which I think is also easy for me to iterate my design in a short time.
